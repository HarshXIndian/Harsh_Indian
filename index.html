Full Answer

Why I choose "Indian" over caste identity

I do not write "Indian" because I think I am more Indian than anyone else, or because I want to prove something. I write it because I do not believe in defining myself through caste, and I refuse to let inherited labels decide identity.

For me, the nation comes first, and everything else comes after that. Many people proudly attach their caste to their name and then celebrate the achievements of someone from that same caste as if it is a shared victory.

But then one honest question follows: when someone from that same caste is involved in rape, corruption, or betrayal of the country, why does that collective pride suddenly go silent? If people are ready to claim the credit, should they not also face the accountability?

That is why "Indian" feels far more honest to me than any caste title. And if someone says a caste name brings support from its own people, then "Indian" is an even greater strength, because the nation is larger than any caste.
